There’s a lie circulating that content marketing is just about creating content, and that if you produce content the world will be yours. Content marketing isn’t only about creation—you must market the content you’re creating. The Content Growth Framework helps fix a leak in your business by combining research, creation, distribution, and optimization. Start with audience research: understand customers, their habits, and behaviors, plus what competitors are bidding on and what channels they use. Look at media habits to determine where your audience spends time (newsletters, blogs, podcasts, etc.). Use tools like SparkToro or Audience to gather these insights and identify content market fit—where every publish will resonate with your audience. Do community research in relevant spaces to see which stories get upvotes and backlinks, guiding which content to create. With that insight, you’ll know which channels to distribute on, including social platforms, newsletters, podcasts, and sponsorships or guest appearances. Then implement paid media, remarketing, and retargeting to maximize reach and engagement, ensuring you tap into content market fit. Don’t stop there: revisit older content two months, a quarter, or a year later and optimize it for CRO (conversion rate optimization), SEO (search engine optimization), and GEO (generative engine optimization). Continuously optimize old content to sustain ROI. In short: research your audience, create what they want, distribute relentlessly, and optimize existing content to keep driving ROI for years.
