Everyone’s asking, “How should I use AI in marketing?”



I think that’s the wrong question. It lacks nuance and if anything, sounds like you're trying to shoehorn one thing into another.



A better question is: Which parts of marketing should AI touch first — and which parts should stay human?



My take:



• Content creation: maybe; use caution.



AI is useful for outlining and rough first drafts. But the second you need a point of view with teeth, you're only going to get statistically likely word sequences from an LLM. Use that, and you'll be publishing the same overly polished, lukewarm sludge everyone else is.



And in a zero-click world, sameness means you don’t get noticed, amplified, or cited.



• Audience research: ok, but don’t get lazy.



AI can help synthesize large pieces of information quickly. Great. But it doesn't replace understanding where your audience spends time, who they trust, and what actually influences their decisions.



Use it to move faster. Don’t use it to outsource your comprehension.



• Distribution and repurposing: yes, but supervise closely.



This is where AI is a lot more useful. Turning a webinar, interview, or podcast into clips, social posts, and draft writeups is tedious work. AI is good at tedious work, especially when it's directly fed the inputs.



The human job is deciding what’s actually worth amplifying, and holding yourself accountable for the decisions you make.



• Measurement and attribution: be careful.



AI can spot patterns fast. But if your inputs are messy, your outputs will be well-said nonsense. I’m especially cautious here because LLMs are trained to generate plausible language, not reliable analysis. Beware of hallucinated numbers and overconfident conclusions. 



The point is, AI is strongest when it helps marketers execute faster.

It’s weakest when people expect it to replace taste, judgment, or strategy.



This is also why I think more marketers need practical conversations about where AI belongs in the workflow — and clearer standards for using it responsibly at work.
