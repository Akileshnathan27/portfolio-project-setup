Build vs buy is the biggest challenge to GTM tech in 2026.



For 10+ years selling SaaS, I'd laugh when a customer said "we might build this ourselves." Closed lost reason = delusional customer. They'd be back in 6 months when the project failed.



That was before LLMs.



Now every buyer is asking "can't we build this?" with real confidence. 



So how will GTM tech providers survive?



Clay found one approach.



They picked the side of the builder. Changed the verb. We \*build\* in Clay. We \*use\* our other GTM tech. This is why Clay hit $100M so fast. They are riding the wave, not fighting it.



I think packaged solutions can win too. 



But the bar is much higher. Needs to be obviously different, better, faster, more reliable. Keeps getting better as the world changes.



The middle is dying.



You're either the platform where people build, or you're solving it better than they can build it themselves.



I feel this pressure every day building in the space. 



The lazy middle won't make it to 2027.

\*\*Why saved:\*\* This post identifies a major structural shift in the SAAS industry where AI is lowering the barrier for companies to build internal tools, forcing SaaS providers to offer deeper, more defensible value.
