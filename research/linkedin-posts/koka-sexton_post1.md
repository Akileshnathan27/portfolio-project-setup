Your LinkedIn engagement isn’t “content performance.”

It’s a \*\*buyer intelligence feed\*\* most teams completely ignore. 👀



While marketing celebrates impressions…

The smartest teams are quietly building \*\*pipeline reports from comments and likes.\*\*






Every post reveals signals:



👤 Founders testing ideas

⚙️ Operators with active problems

💰 VCs watching a space

🎯 Buyers warming themselves up



But almost nobody tracks it.



Instead teams do the corporate equivalent of:



“Wow, this post got 120 likes.”

“Nice.” 👍



Meanwhile someone in those likes:



• engaged \*\*3 times in 2 weeks\*\*

• works at your \*\*ICP company\*\*

• follows \*\*multiple people on your team\*\*

• just \*\*raised funding\*\*



That’s not engagement.



That’s a \*\*buying signal wearing sweatpants.\*\*



So we built a simple workflow:



Step 1: Export LinkedIn activity

Step 2: Analyze posts + repeat engagers

Step 3: Map cross-profile engagement

Step 4: Identify warm accounts

Step 5: Turn signals → plays





Example plays:



🎯 \*\*Play 01 — Founder Signal\*\*

Trigger: founder or exec engages

Action: connect + reference the post they reacted to



🔥 \*\*Play 02 — Multi-Touch Warm Up\*\*

Trigger: someone engages 2+ times in 30 days

Action: comment on their post → DM within 7 days



🧭 \*\*Play 03 — Account Mapping\*\*

Trigger: multiple people from one company engage

Action: map roles → pick entry point → ask for intro



Now LinkedIn isn’t “social.”
