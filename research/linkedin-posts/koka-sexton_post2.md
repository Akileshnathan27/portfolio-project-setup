Let's take a moment and focus on how PandaDoc is posting like a SaaS company. But the data says they could be posting like a movement. 🚀



255,405 followers.

12 posts in 30 days.

685 total engagements.

\~57 engagements per post.



At this size?



That’s not reach.

That’s under-leverage. ⚠️



Here’s what jumps out:



🔥 The top-performing post wasn’t about features.

It was about layoffs + hiring.



242 engagements.

+323% vs average.



Why?



Because it tapped into emotion + timing.

Not “document automation.”

Not “AI prompts.”

People.



Now look at the pattern:



📸 Images win (117 avg)

🎥 Video underutilized (45 avg)

🧠 Product content performs best (133 avg)

🗣 0% author reply rate



That last one hurts.



You can’t run a “Town Hall” interaction mode

and then not show up to the meeting.



87% first-time engagers tells me this page has discovery power.

But only 13% repeats?



That means awareness is happening.

Community isn’t.



And here’s the real kicker:



The worst-performing posts?



AI prompt downloads.

Generic CTAs.

Link-out heavy.



Translation:



Utility ≠ emotional gravity.



PandaDoc wins when it positions itself as:



✔️ The company helping teams sell smarter

✔️ The brand hiring when others freeze

✔️ The platform reducing friction in chaos



It loses when it sounds like every other martech account.



At 250K+ followers, you don’t need more posts.



You need sharper angles.



Less “Get the guide.”

More “Here’s the shift happening in sales ops.”



Less link.

More leadership.



Because Ashley Kemper, the audience is already there.



The only question is:



Will PandaDoc keep acting like software…
