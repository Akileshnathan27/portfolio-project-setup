A lot of business comes down to WHO you know.



And people will try anything to network.



But, because they're so desperate, they often resort to "hacks" and "tricks" and bad cold emails and handing out business cards with some sales pitch.



There's a faster way to build a top 1% network.



1\. Be the person who \*actually\* follows up.



2\. Remember people's names and address them as such.



3\. Celebrate other people's wins (LinkedIn is a great place for that).



4\. Show up when it's "inconvenient," like when people desperately need help.



Of course, these aren't the only things that matter. 

And yes, having a compelling life/business is great.



But nearly everyone I know skips the basics in favor of trying whatever newfangled tip the "experts" are sharing.



Want one simple tip you can execute today to help build your network?



\- Find someone you admire.

\- Read some of their recent content like a newsletter.

\- Reach out and tell them why it was meaningful.

\- Don't ask for anything.



That's it.



Do that once a day for 365 days. 

See what happens to your network.



\---



\*\*Why saved:\*\* It reframes networking as a byproduct of public expertise and consistent content, rather than a manual, one-on-one outreach process.
